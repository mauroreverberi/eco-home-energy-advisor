Seasonal Energy Management

Energy needs change with the seasons. Adjusting habits and settings each season keeps comfort high while keeping bills and emissions low.

Summer:
- Raise the cooling setpoint and rely on ceiling fans and night ventilation.
- Pre-cool during midday solar and coast through the late-afternoon price peak.
- Close blinds on sun-facing windows to reduce heat gain; this lowers cooling load significantly.

Winter:
- Lower the heating setpoint and add layers; set back the temperature overnight and when away.
- Let in winter sun during the day and close curtains at night to retain heat.
- Service heating equipment before the cold season for peak efficiency.

Spring and fall (shoulder seasons):
- Use natural ventilation and open windows instead of HVAC whenever outdoor temperatures are comfortable.
- These mild months are ideal for running deferred high-energy tasks (deep cleaning appliances, EV trips) because HVAC load is low.

Seasonal solar:
- Solar generation is highest in summer and lowest in winter. Plan higher self-consumption goals in summer and lean more on efficiency and load-shifting in winter.

Seasonal demand awareness:
- Utilities often have higher peak rates in summer (cooling) and sometimes winter (heating). Check seasonal tariff changes and adjust scheduling accordingly.
